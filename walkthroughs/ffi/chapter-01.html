Final Fantasy I (Pixel Remaster) — Chapter 1: Cornelia & The First Steps

Get settled in Cornelia, make smart early purchases, and set up for your first dungeon.

Game: FFI (Pixel Remaster) Chapter: 1 Estimated time: 20–45 min Spoiler level: Low
Goal: Leave Cornelia with enough gear and supplies to reach the Temple of Fiends comfortably.

On this page

Goals Party Notes (Quick) Shopping Priorities Step-by-step Tips & Mistakes Next Chapter

Goals

  • Learn the “town loop”: shops → inn → save → head out.
  • Buy a few meaningful upgrades (don’t waste early gold).
  • Test fights just outside town and adjust if needed.

Party Notes (Quick)

General rule

  • Put tougher jobs in the front (they’ll take more hits).
  • If you’re taking heavy damage, prioritize armor next.
  • If fights are slow, prioritize weapons first.

Pixel Remaster habit

  • Keep your HP topped between fights; unlucky turns happen early.
  • Don’t be afraid to return to town — it’s efficient, not “cheating.”

Shopping Priorities

  • Weapons first for your main damage dealer(s).
  • Armor second if you’re losing too much HP per fight.
  • Buy a small stack of Potions (enough to survive bad luck, not enough to go broke).
Simple check: If you’re using 2–3 Potions every fight, you’re undergeared.

Step-by-step

1) Explore Cornelia

  • Talk to NPCs to get basic direction and context.
  • Locate the Inn and the main shops (weapons/armor/items).

2) Buy key upgrades

  • Upgrade 1–2 weapons (frontliners / main attacker first).
  • Upgrade armor if needed (especially for whoever is taking the most hits).
  • Buy Potions (small stack).

3) Test combat just outside town

  • Fight a few battles near Cornelia.
  • Decide which problem you have: damage too low (weapon) or taking too much damage (armor).
  • If it feels rough: return to town, rest, and buy one more key upgrade.

4) Set up for the first dungeon push

  • Rest at the Inn so you begin at full strength.
  • Save your game before leaving town.
  • Head out toward your first main objective (covered next chapter).

Tips & Mistakes

  • Don’t overbuy items. Gear upgrades reduce item usage.
  • Retreating is smart. Early FF1 is about safe, consistent progress.
  • Focus fire. Removing one enemy fast prevents surprise KOs.
