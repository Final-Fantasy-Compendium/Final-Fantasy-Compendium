Final Fantasy I — Chapter 1: Cornelia & The First Steps

Get your bearings in Cornelia, gear up smartly, and set up for the game’s first real dungeon.

Game: FFI Chapter: 1 Estimated time: 20–45 min Spoiler level: Low

Goals

  • Learn the town loop (inn, shops, saving/resting).
  • Buy a solid set of early gear without wasting money.
  • Head out of Cornelia and reach the first “real” danger zone safely.

Prep Checklist

Recommended

  • Rest at the Inn when low on HP/MP.
  • Buy a small stack of Potions (don’t overbuy).
  • Upgrade at least your frontliners’ weapons first.

Optional

  • Fight a few battles outside town if you’re struggling.
  • Save before leaving town after shopping.
  • Test your spell/attack rhythm to avoid wasted turns.
Money tip: In FF1, early money feels tight. Prioritize a couple of meaningful upgrades over “a little bit of everything.”

Step-by-step Walkthrough

1) Cornelia: Talk, shop, and stabilize

  • Walk around town and talk to NPCs to get directions and context.
  • Find the Inn and the shops (weapons/armor/items).
  • Buy a modest amount of healing items so you can survive bad luck in early fights.

2) Gear priorities (simple and effective)

  • Weapons first for your main damage dealer(s) (usually your front line).
  • Armor second if you’re taking too much damage per fight.
  • If you have a caster, don’t blow your whole budget trying to “max” magic early—gear keeps you alive.

3) Leave Cornelia and test your party

  • Head outside and do a few fights close to town.
  • Watch how much HP you lose per encounter and how fast you can finish fights.
  • If fights feel rough, return to town, rest, and consider one more key upgrade.

4) Push toward your first objective

  • Once your party can win consistently without burning all resources, continue onward.
  • Save when you can, and don’t be afraid to retreat to town to reset.

Loot & Missables

  • Nothing “permanently missable” in this opening stretch for most versions, but early gold efficiency matters.
  • Rule of thumb: if you find yourself buying more healing than gear, you’re undergeared.

Tips & Common Mistakes

  • Don’t overbuy items early. Gear upgrades reduce how many items you need.
  • Retreating is smart in FF1. Town is your reset button.
  • If battles are dragging, prioritize a weapon upgrade before grinding levels.
